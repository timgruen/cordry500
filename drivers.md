## El Jefe · P1 · 1 driver

**Group Story:** Palou starts from pole as defending champion. There's only one boss at this race.

**#10 Alex Palou 🇪🇸 | W**
Three-time IndyCar champion who won the 2025 Indy 500 from pole. If the race runs clean, he's the most likely person to repeat — and the most expensive group to own.

## Fuel Economy · P2 · 1 driver

**Group Story:** Rossi won his first Indy 500 by literally coasting across the finish line after his car ran dry.

**#20 Alexander Rossi 🇺🇸 | W**
Won his debut Indy 500 in 2016 by running out of fuel and still winning. Former F1 reserve driver who found his calling at 500 miles per race.

---

## Chi-Town Hustle · P3 · 1 driver

**Group Story:** Malukas is Chicago's own hometown racer, grinding his way toward a breakthrough win.

**#12 David Malukas 🇺🇸**
Born and raised in Chicago — one of the few American-born title contenders in the field. Finished runner-up in the 2025 Indy 500 and is absolutely due for that top step.

## Country Roads · P4–5 · 2 drivers

**Group Story:** Rosenqvist's #60 car sports a Morgan Wallen country music livery — the most visually charismatic car at the Speedway.

**#60 Felix Rosenqvist 🇸🇪**
Finished 2nd in the 2023 Indy 500 and has been knocking on the door ever since. The Morgan Wallen livery means fans have been taking photos of this car all week.

**#14 Santino Ferrucci 🇺🇸**
Rhode Islander who has quietly become one of the best pure oval racers in the field. Finished 3rd in 2023 and races without fear in the heaviest traffic on the grid.

---

## Missile Launch · P6–7 · 2 drivers

**Group Story:** O'Ward's nickname is "The Mexican Missile" — and Simpson is launching his IndyCar career from P7.

**#5 Pato O'Ward 🇲🇽**
Has finished 2nd at Indy twice and is statistically overdue for a win. His McLaren deal even scored him a test in a real Formula 1 car. The most frustrated nearly-man at the Brickyard.

**#8 Kyffin Simpson 🇰🇾**
Carries one of the most unique passports in motorsport — a Cayman Islander making his third Indy 500 start. Won the Indy Lights championship before joining the Chip Ganassi powerhouse.

---

## Ice Ice Baby · P8–10 · 3 drivers

**Group Story:** Dixon's nickname is "The Iceman" — two ice-cold Kiwis and one famously charismatic American walk into a race.

**#23 Conor Daly 🇺🇸 🏴󠁵󠁳󠁩󠁮󠁿**
Son of TV racing analyst Derek Daly. Once crowdfunded his entire Indy 500 entry from fans. Universally considered the most fun driver in the paddock — and he's quicker than you think.

**#3 Scott McLaughlin 🇳🇿**
Three-time V8 Supercars champion who traded Australian touring car wars for American open-wheel. Won the 2021 Indy 500 Rookie of the Year and improves every single year.

**#9 Scott Dixon 🇳🇿 | W**
Six-time IndyCar champion with a career spanning over 20 years at Indy. At 45, he still doesn't make mistakes. If the race gets strategic, Dixon wins from anywhere.

---

## Home Turf · P11–13 · 3 drivers

**Group Story:** Ed Carpenter lives in Indianapolis and races nowhere else. This is literally his home track, front yard, and entire annual racing calendar.

**#76 Rinus VeeKay 🇳🇱**
"The Flying Dutchman" — became the youngest IndyCar polesitter at age 20. Has the raw speed to embarrass championship favorites, and ECR knows this track cold.

**#75 Takuma Sato 🇯🇵 | W×2**
Two-time winner (2017, 2020) who races with the spirit of a kamikaze pilot. Former F1 driver. At 49, either wins or crashes spectacularly — there is no middle ground with Sato.

**#33 Ed Carpenter 🇺🇸 🏴󠁵󠁳󠁩󠁮󠁿**
The only owner-driver who races exclusively at Indianapolis. Three poles. Zero wins. The most haunted man at the Speedway — and the most dangerous on his home track.

---

## Spider-Man's Web · P14–17 · 4 drivers

**Group Story:** Hélio Castroneves earned "Spider-Man" by climbing the catch fencing after every win. At 50, he's chasing an unprecedented 5th — and still doing it with a smile.

**#06 Hélio Castroneves 🇧🇷 | W×4**
Four-time winner (2001, 2002, 2009, 2021) and also a Dancing with the Stars champion. Nobody in motorsport history has won here more than four times — yet.

**#21 Christian Rasmussen 🇩🇰**
The quietest driver at the Speedway — until race day. Finished 6th in the 2025 Indy 500 and is methodically building toward a podium finish with Andretti Global.

**#66 Marcus Armstrong 🇳🇿**
Named IndyCar Rookie of the Year in 2023 and has been getting faster ever since. Part of the wave of New Zealanders quietly dominating American open-wheel racing.

**#28 Marcus Ericsson 🇸🇪 | W**
Won the 2022 Indy 500 by being in the right place during a perfectly-timed late caution. Knows that patience and strategy win at Indianapolis — and he's proven it.

---

## Sleeping Giants · P18–24 · 7 drivers

**Group Story:** Back-to-back winner Newgarden (P23) and 2018 champion Power (P19) are buried in this group. The most dangerous seven cars on the grid — do not sleep on Sleeping Giants.

**#7 Christian Lundgaard 🇩🇰**
Underrated Dane who has been quicker at Indy every year. Quietly one of the most talented drivers in the field without the results to prove it — yet.

**#26 Will Power 🇦🇺 | W**
Holds the all-time Indy 500 qualifying speed record and won in 2018. Qualified P19. Do not tell him he can't win from the back — he'll prove you wrong by lap 100.

**#6 Nolan Siegel 🇺🇸**
Was running inside the top 10 in the final laps of the 2025 race when he crashed out. That's called motivation. Big McLaren talent in a fast car with a point to prove.

**#45 Louis Foster 🇬🇧**
2025 IndyCar Rookie of the Year making his Indianapolis 500 debut. British-made, McLaren-powered, and arriving with a chip on his shoulder the size of a Cadillac engine.

**#31 Ryan Hunter-Reay 🇺🇸 | W**
2014 champion in one of sport's great emotional stories — his wife Andrea battled breast cancer the year he won. One of the most beloved figures in American racing history.

**#2 Josef Newgarden 🇺🇸 | W×2**
Back-to-back champion in 2023 and 2024, starting from P23. Penske's most complete driver. In IndyCar terms, P23 is P3 for Newgarden — he'll be in contention by lap 50.

**#18 Romain Grosjean 🇫🇷**
Survived a 200mph fireball at the 2020 Bahrain F1 GP that left him with severe hand burns. Reinvented his career in IndyCar and races with, as he says, "nothing left to fear."

## Long Shot Lottery · P25–33 · 9 drivers

**Group Story:** Starting dead last through 25th — but someone wins from the back of the grid every single year. This is your chaos hedge, your conversation starter, and your wildcard.

**#27 Kyle Kirkwood 🇺🇸**
Consistent Andretti improver who quietly collects top-10s when conditions go sideways. Starting P25 — chaos is absolutely his friend.

**#11 Katherine Legge 🇬🇧**
British barrier-breaker who also attempted the Indy 500/Coca-Cola 600 double in 2026. More racing miles in 24 hours than almost any human alive.

**#47 Mick Schumacher 🇩🇪 | R**
Son of seven-time F1 champion Michael Schumacher, making his Indy 500 debut. The most famous surname in motorsport takes on the biggest race in America.

**#15 Graham Rahal 🇺🇸**
Son of 1986 Indy 500 winner Bobby Rahal, running his own family's team. Has been agonizingly close to following his father's footsteps for over a decade.

**#19 Dennis Hauger 🇳🇴 | R**
Norwegian F2 standout making his oval racing debut in Prema's first-ever Indy 500 entry. Zero oval experience before this week. Chaos potential: extremely high.

**#51 Jacob Abel 🇺🇸 🏴󠁵󠁳󠁩󠁮󠁿 | R**
Born and raised in Indianapolis, running his own family-funded car in the race he grew up watching from the infield. The purest Indy 500 story in the 2026 field.

**#77 Sting Ray Robb 🇺🇸**
His actual given name is Sting Ray, named after his grandfather's Corvette. Idaho native racing for the legendary AJ Foyt. The most American sentence in motorsport.

**#4 Caio Collet 🇧🇷 | R**
Brazilian rookie who qualified impressively before a technical violation sent him to the back row. McLaren's wildcard with plenty of speed and everything to prove.

**#24 Jack Harvey 🇬🇧**
British journeyman quietly starting at Indy every year since 2017. Never the loudest, never the flashiest — just reliable, consistent, and perpetually underappreciated.
